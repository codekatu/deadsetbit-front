Empathic Building

Summary

We helped TietoEVRY and later Haltian to develop the technical side of their Empathic Building product line as well as design the overall product concept. From initial concept MVP launched at Slush 2017 to current day slew of product lines targeting offices, hospitals, warehouses and more, we have continued our strong subcontracting relationship and offered our senior expertise over a number of different technical domains starting from designing the overall architecture of the IoT platform to implementation of individual end-user web applications and graphic design for over 8 years already.

Highlights

Highly performant interfaces

From a REST API capable of handling +300 000 000 requests each month to enabling real-time communications efficiently between the server and clients through web socket and MQTT technology.

World-class visualizations

From visualizing individual buildings to entire surrounding areas, our in-house developed WebGL based DSB Web Application Engine ensures unparalled 2D & 3D visualization capabilities within the browser environment.

Architecture for the whole stack

Designing and implementing both backend and frontend architectures from zero to hero while making sure both are kept adaptable enough for the usual pivots of a startup company.

Connectivity to third party systems

Implementing +20 integrations to third party platforms, from Office 365 to real-time location systems (RTLS) and everything in between. Connectivity is key.

First of Its Kind, An End-User Interface For Buildings

The futuristic idea of visualizing invisible building data for the actual end-users of the building was not common place in 2016. Together with TietoEVRY’s startup incubator, we implemented a fully-functioning MVP in 6 months time, after which the product was offficially launched at the international tech and startup event Slush in 2017 with glowing fanfare and interest generated.

Given the novelty of the concept and absence of any comparable implementations, we had to come up with new and innovative approaches for implementing the required features. This included using our proprietary game engine to render buildings within the end-user's browser environment quickly with extremely low memory overhead and load times. Throughout, end-user experience remained the cornerstone of our technical and product design efforts.
