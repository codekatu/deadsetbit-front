About the client

Haltian is a Finnish IoT-company that specializes in making simple and effective IoT technology to streamline everyday working life. Haltian’s Empathic Building solution is the cornerstone of their SaaS offering, covering different use-cases in office, hospital and warehouse environments, where visualization of IoT data is of paramount interest to the end-users of the building. To date, Haltian has raised over 40 million euros in funding. The Empathic Building solution is trusted by companies globally. Customers include companies such as Uniper (DE), Telefonica (ES), Shimizu Corporation (JP), Telia (SE) & National Health Service (UK).

First of Its Kind, An End-User Interface For Buildings

Empathic Building makes building data visible for the end-users of the building, helping them more effectively navigate around the taken, which are reserved in the future and where colleagues are workplace. Users can see which rooms and desks are currently at the moment. It achieves this by combining data from different IoT-sensors, cloud services such as Microsoft’s Office365 and dedicated real-time location services (RTLS). Users are able to interact with the building and improve it by leaving service tickets and feedback in specific locations, thereby also making management of the building easier. For facility management the solution enables the ability to generate reports on the usage of floors even down to individual tables, rooms and areas to better improve the working place and different areas of it. Whether an office building, a hospital or a warehouse, the versatile solution is able to cater to many different use-cases and sectors of business.

How we did it

The futuristic idea of visualizing invisible building data for the actual end-users of the building was not commonplace in 2016. Together with TietoEVRY’s startup incubator, we implemented a fully-functioning MVP in 6 months time, after which the product was officially launched at the international tech and startup event Slush in 2017.

Given the novelty of the concept and absence of any comparable implementations, we had to come up with new and innovative approaches for implementing the required features. This included using our proprietary game engine to render buildings within the end-user's browser environment quickly with extremely low memory overhead and load times. To support our end-goals, we created versatile and extendable formats for storing information about the buildings themselves, down to individual IoT-sensor measurement formats.

Throughout, end-user experience remained the cornerstone of our technical and product design efforts.
